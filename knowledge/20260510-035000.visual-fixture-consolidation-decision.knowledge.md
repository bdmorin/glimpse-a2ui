# Visual Fixture Consolidation — Decision Record

## Note to future-Brian (and future-agent)

You're reading this because you're either:

1. About to re-bless goldens after the next theme/renderer change and the cost made you tilt your head, OR
2. Investigating a visual regression and wondering why the harness is shaped the way it is, OR
3. Doing routine repo maintenance and trying to understand what artifact decisions sit unreviewed.

Past-me considered consolidating from 9 → 3 fixtures, didn't, and **deferred deliberately** with a trigger condition. The reasoning is here so you can second-guess it from a stronger position than I was in. If you decide to keep 9, fine. If you go to 3, also fine. Just don't decide blind.

## Current state (as of `v0.8.7-mad-scientist-garage`, hash `7be8486d67c6`)

The harness has 9 fixtures, one per component category:

```
test/__snapshots__/7be8486d67c6/
  button.png              card.png
  card-text.png           checkbox.png
  icon.png                modal.png
  multiple-choice.png     slider.png
  tabs.png                text-field-form.png
```

Each renders a small, isolated invocation of one component on a 480×320 canvas (test-mode locked geometry). Diff threshold: 0.0000% across all 9 after the design-track + iconography merge.

The mechanic: every meaningful change to `src/a2glimpse-host.html` shifts the host bundle's hash, which the harness uses as the snapshot directory name. Re-bless means:
1. Capture all 9 fresh at the new hash.
2. Remove the old hash dir.
3. Stage + commit.

That cost lands proportional to the fixture count. With 9, it's noticeable but absorbable. With 30, the re-bless friction would push back on theme iteration; that pressure would be wrong.

## The question Brian raised

> couldn't we put all those elements in one canvas and do fewer goldens?

The intuition: a single composite "kitchen-sink" canvas with every component visible in one frame would cut re-bless cost by 8/9. One `composite.png`, done.

It's a real trade-off, not a free win.

## The trade-off, named

| Current: 9 isolated fixtures | Proposed: 1 kitchen-sink |
|---|---|
| ✅ Per-component blame on regressions | ❌ Localized bugs harder to attribute — 2px drift in a 1500×900 image, hunt for which component drifted |
| ❌ Re-bless is 9× per merge | ✅ Re-bless is 1× per merge |
| ✅ Layout-context-free — Modal regressions don't taint Slider goldens | ✅ Composite captures real interactions (Row gaps, Card-in-Column nesting) |
| ✅ Small image → small diff math (pixel-cheap) | ❌ Large image → larger pixel surface, slower diff |
| ❌ Doesn't catch interaction regressions (e.g., a Card-in-Modal layout bug) | ✅ Catches interaction regressions |
| ✅ Per-fixture B1-filing citability ("CheckBox renders wrong, see fixture X") | ❌ Citing "the kitchen-sink shows the bug" is weaker provenance for an upstream issue |

The two columns above are NOT symmetric. **Re-bless cost is linear in fixture count; debugging cost is non-linear in fixture-canvas-area.** A 9× re-bless reduction is a real ergonomic win. A 9× debugging-attribution penalty when something breaks is a real ergonomic loss. Which dominates depends on the rate of intentional theme iteration vs the rate of accidental component regressions.

## The proposed shape — 9 → 3, not 9 → 1

The right point on the curve is probably:

1. **One kitchen-sink composite** (`kitchen-sink.png`) — every theme-relevant component on one canvas in realistic layout (Card-with-Row-of-Buttons, TextField-above-Slider, etc.). Captures: token swaps, shadow stamps, primary/secondary differentiation, hover/focus, code chip treatment. **~80% of "did the theme survive?" coverage in one image.**
2. **`modal.png`** — kept isolated. Modal has its own dialog scope and the auto-open wrapperPatch. Test-mode auto-open behavior is regression-prone. Worth its own canvas.
3. **`upstream-bugs.png`** — combines MultipleChoice + CheckBox in a single canvas. These are the components currently filed upstream against `google/A2UI` (B1 filings). Per-filing citability matters; isolated-from-the-rest matters less. Combined here keeps both hot.

That's 9 → 3, **~67% re-bless cost reduction**, while preserving:

- Modal isolation (it's where most "weird state" lives)
- B1-citable upstream-bug fixtures (CheckBox stretched-input, MultipleChoice empty-array crash)
- Composite catches systemic theme regressions in one diff

Components dropped as standalone fixtures in this proposal: Slider, Card, Text, Tabs, Icon, TextField, Button. Their visuals show up in the kitchen-sink composite. Their per-component bugs (if any arise) would be caught structurally, not pixel-perfectly.

## Why I didn't pull the trigger now

Three reasons, in priority order:

1. **The trade-off is unproven on this codebase.** We just shipped one cumulative re-bless. Proposing a harness reshape based on n=1 of expensive re-bless is reasoning from anecdote. The next 2-3 re-bless cycles will tell us more about whether 9 fixtures are actually the bottleneck or whether something else (capture environment, mcporter race) is the load-bearing slowness.
2. **Next re-bless trigger is already known and imminent.** Brian explicitly flagged: "the next golden will be after design-track applies some much needed styling to the canvas." So we don't have long to wait. Better to observe one more cycle with the current 9 in place, *then* make the consolidation call with two data points instead of one.
3. **The B1 filings cite the current per-fixture goldens by path.** `knowledge/filings/20260509-192800.checkbox-stretched-input.filing.md` references `test/__snapshots__/<hash>/checkbox.png`. Consolidating before those filings clear Google's CLA and get assigned issue numbers means rewriting filings. Wasteful.

## The trigger condition — when this decision should be revisited

Pull the trigger on the consolidation when ALL of these are true:

- [ ] At least one more cumulative re-bless has happened since 2026-05-10 (so we have n=2 cost data points).
- [ ] B1 filings have either been filed and assigned issue numbers (so they can cite the new fixture shape) OR closed out as wontfix on Google's side.
- [ ] No active theme/renderer iteration is mid-flight (consolidating during active iteration causes diff-noise on top of intended-diff).
- [ ] You can articulate, in one sentence, what specific pain you're trying to relieve. ("I'm tired of waiting for re-bless" is a valid sentence. "It just feels like too many fixtures" is not.)

If all four are checked, dispatch a sub-agent with the proposed 9 → 3 shape. The dispatch prompt should include:
- The rationale from this doc
- Explicit list of which fixtures merge into kitchen-sink
- Modal stays isolated (with auto-open patch behavior preserved)
- MultipleChoice + CheckBox combine into upstream-bugs.png
- Re-bless protocol from `20260509-160946.agent-dispatch-procedure.knowledge.md`

## What past-me might have gotten wrong

Three things future-me should suspect:

1. **The 80/20 estimate ("80% of theme regression coverage in one composite") is intuition, not measurement.** Could be 95%; could be 50%. Don't trust the number.
2. **"Modal stays isolated" might be over-protective.** If the kitchen-sink can include a triggered modal (auto-opened in test-mode), one fixture might cover both. Worth a small spike.
3. **The B1 filing argument might be a red herring.** Even with consolidated fixtures, filing-time citations could just embed the relevant crop. The "rewriting filings" objection assumes a rigid filing format that may not actually be rigid.

If future-me reconsiders any of these, the answer might be 9 → 2 (kitchen-sink + upstream-bugs) or even 9 → 1 (everything composite). Don't anchor on 3.

## What future-me might wish past-me had done

Possible regrets, ranked by likely-pain:

1. **"Wish you'd dispatched a 30-minute spike to actually measure the kitchen-sink coverage."** — would have produced one composite fixture, run the harness against the design-track diff, and counted how many of the 9 component diffs would have been detectable in the composite. Real data, ~30 min of agent time. We didn't because the cumulative re-bless arc was already complete and adding a measurement loop felt like scope creep.
2. **"Wish you'd at least added the kitchen-sink as a 10th fixture (no-op for current 9, additive coverage)."** — minimal-risk move; lets future-me run measurements without committing to consolidation. Considered; deferred for the same n=1 reason.
3. **"Wish you'd set the consolidation trigger looser."** — the four-condition gate above might be too cautious. If the next re-bless is painful enough, future-me will feel the cost more sharply and may want to act on n=1 data. That's fine — gate is suggestion, not law.
